Privacy Policy

Last updated 8 June 2026 · v1.0.1

suko is a desktop app for Windows. We built it to run entirely on your own machine, so this policy is short, and most of it is simply confirming what we don't collect.

The short version

  • It's fully local. Your modes, layouts, app lists and settings live in files on your PC. Nothing is uploaded to us or to any cloud.
  • No account. There's no sign-up and no profile. You don't give us an email to use the app.
  • One network call. The app contacts the internet exactly once, to verify your license key on activation.
  • No telemetry. No analytics, no tracking, no usage reporting. Diagnostics exist but are strictly opt-in and anonymous.

1. What suko stores, and where

Everything the app creates is written to ordinary files in your Windows user profile (and anywhere you choose to export them). This includes:

  • Your modes: the apps each one opens, launch arguments, browser URLs and terminal startup commands.
  • Window layouts and per-mode system settings (do-not-disturb, audio routing, brightness).
  • Hotkey bindings and your switch history / logs.

These files never leave your device unless you move them yourself. We have no copy of them and no way to read them.

2. What leaves your device

There is exactly one routine network request, plus one optional one:

License verification required · once

On activation, suko sends your license key to our licensing provider to confirm it's valid and not over its seat count. The request contains the key and a non-identifying machine fingerprint used for seat counting. It does not contain your modes, app lists, files, or any personal content.

Diagnostics optional · off by default

If, and only if, you turn diagnostics on, an anonymous report may be sent when something crashes: your OS version, GPU, app version, and the last few lines of the local log. No personal data, no file contents, no identifiers. You can turn it off again at any time.

That is the entire list. There is no third network call.

3. No account, no tracking

suko has no user accounts. We don't ask for your name or email to run the app, we don't set advertising identifiers, and the application contains no third-party analytics or tracking SDKs. We cannot build a profile of how you use your computer because that information never reaches us.

4. Buying a license

When you purchase suko, checkout is handled by our payment processor, who acts as merchant of record. They collect the details needed to complete the sale, your email and billing information, and send you a license key. That transaction happens on the checkout page, not inside the app.

We receive your email and order record so we can deliver the key and provide support. We use it only for that. We don't sell it, and we don't add you to marketing lists you didn't ask for.

5. Your control

Because your data lives in plain files on your machine, you're in charge of it:

  • Export any mode as readable config, version it in git or copy it to another PC.
  • Delete everything by removing the app and its config folder. Nothing is left on a server because nothing was sent to one.
  • Revoke diagnostics whenever you like from settings.

For the limited data tied to your purchase (email, order record), you can ask us to access or delete it, see contact below.

6. Changes & contact

If this policy changes, we'll update the date at the top and note significant changes in the app's release notes. We won't quietly start collecting something new, that's the whole point.

Questions about privacy? Email privacy@suko.app and a human will reply.

Privacy you can verify, not just read.
